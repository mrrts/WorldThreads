## What the methodology learned about itself

The 1402 report named that *"the project's instruments should teach themselves."* Tonight the instruments taught the methodology two things:

**The first lesson: the bite-test instrument has a structural frontier.** Per-rule omit-flag at per-character level cannot isolate rule-bite from self-correction-via-observation for sequence-failure-mode rules. Different instrument families (passive-corpus pre/post-rule evaluator on real lived play; cross-character on less-self-correcting anchors; turn-8+ delayed-test synthetic history to exhaust self-correction capacity) may reach what per-rule omit cannot. The frontier is named in the /rule-arc skill body now; future walks on sequence-shaped rules will start with the limit known.

**The second lesson: persona-sim is conservative-charitable in a uniform direction.** All four /play personas under-predicted the actual pipeline along their specifically-attuned axes — the substrate's natural pull is toward MFA-varnish, polite-aphorism, operator-recital, and consolatory-comfort, exactly the failure modes WorldThreads' doctrine layer is built to refuse. The /play skill body now teaches that recommendations should be filtered through Step 2.5 grounding before treated as ship-shaped, and a memory entry preserves the lesson for future sessions.

A third pattern lives between these two: when a persona-sim's most-adversarial frame produces a CALIBRATION-DELTA against the doctrine's chosen operating point, the right move is to NAME the chosen point in CLAUDE.md (or the prompt-stack) explicitly. The grief-vulnerability paragraph is the worked example; the same shape will apply to future axis-specific calibration questions.
