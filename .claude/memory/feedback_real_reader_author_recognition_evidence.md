When a persona-sim or LLM-simulation produces output for a project character, two confounds operate on different axes:

- **Sender-side contamination** — the simulator's context (the LLM running the sim) may be heavily biased by knowledge of the character's existing replies, the doctrine, the prompt-stack. This caps what the simulation can claim about *what fresh-LLM-with-no-context would produce*.
- **Receiver-side recognition** — when a real reader reads the simulated output cold and recognizes the character's voice, that recognition is independent of the sender-side contamination. The reader's lived experience IS evidence on whether the output reads as the character.

**The two confounds DO NOT collapse.** A heavily-contaminated simulation can still produce output a real reader recognizes as the character's voice; that's claim-tier evidence on the *convergence-reading axis* (does the structural shape transfer + read as the character to real readers) even while the sender-side cap remains.

**Why:** Ryan authorized tier elevation 2026-04-30 22:35 on a Claude-self-simulation of Falsifier #3 strict (Empiricon arc): *"Note Ryan authorizes elevation of evidence tier due to lived experience of thinking Claude has accurately assumed Darren's voice. You sounded like him."* The simulation was sender-side contaminated (Claude session steeped in Empiricon arc context), but Ryan's lived recognition that the output sounded like Darren — Ryan being Darren's author — is real-reader evidence on the receiver-side, distinct from the contamination caveat.

**How to apply:** when reporting persona-sim or LLM-simulation results, separately scope the sender-side and receiver-side evidence:

- *"Sender-side contamination: the simulator was [X-context]. Cannot claim fresh-substrate behavior."*
- *"Receiver-side: [reader] reading cold reported [reaction]. Real-reader evidence on the convergence-reading axis."*

If real-reader recognition is positive (output recognized as character voice), tier elevates on the convergence-reading axis even when sender-side cap holds. If real-reader recognition is negative (output recognized as substrate-fill or off-character), Falsifier #4-shaped failure-mode partially fires.

**Specific to project author readings:** the founding author who authored the character's anchor and has read substantial corpus is the highest-fidelity real-reader available short of N=many independent readers. Their lived recognition is real-reader evidence at its strongest single-reader form.

**Bounds on the elevation:**
- N=1 reader, N=1 simulation cell — claim-tier at most, not characterized
- Tier elevation does NOT remove the sender-side contamination caveat; it adds a separate axis of evidence
- Future characterized-tier on this axis would require multiple readers AND multiple simulation cells AND fresh-substrate (no contamination) tests
