# /eureka — a fully-resolved registry becomes an outcome topology

*Generated 2026-04-28 09:15. This run opened immediately after the experiment
registry's `open` shelf was drained to zero. The question was whether that was
just hygiene, or whether the registry had crossed into a different kind of
surface once nothing remained unresolved.*

## The discovery

When `worldcli lab open` returns empty, the experiment registry stops reading
primarily as a **work queue** and starts reading as an **outcome topology**.

That is a real surface change.

The shelf is no longer asking first:

- what still needs attention?

It starts showing:

- what this lab usually confirms
- what it cleanly refutes
- what survives only as discrepant after interpretation

## Why this became visible

The current resolved shelf has a crisp shape:

- 27 `confirmed`
- 12 `refuted`
- 2 `discrepant`
- 0 `open`
- 0 `running`
- 0 `proposed`

That distribution is no longer about workflow management.

It is already a picture of the lab's empirical temperament.

Even before reading individual files, the status topology itself says
something:

- the project does not mostly live in perpetual ambiguity
- it is willing to refute its own hypotheses
- discrepant outcomes are rare enough to stand out as a special class

## Why this is different from hypothesis middleware

Hypothesis middleware governs the minimum honest loop around a question:

- propose
- attach evidence
- resolve

Outcome topology is what appears **after** that loop has run to completion
across the shelf.

Its steering object is not one hypothesis.

It is the lab's self-picture:

- what kinds of bets tend to pay
- what kinds of bets tend to collapse
- where disagreement persists even after serious interpretation

the special case that survives after the queue is drained.

## Why this matters

If the registry were only a convenience layer, draining `open` to zero would be
mostly administrative.

But because the hypothesis slug is the durable unit, and because every resolved
file carries prediction, summary, reports, and evidence attachments, a
fully-resolved shelf becomes a compact empirical mirror of the project's taste
and truthfulness.

It is not just:

- what we tested

It is:

- what kinds of claims this project tends to make honestly
- what kinds of claims it tends to overstate first and learn from later

## Reading

This deepens the `worldcli lab` doctrine one step beyond hypothesis middleware.

Before closure, the registry is a question-carrying surface.

After closure, it becomes a pattern-reading surface.

That means a future session should not look at an all-resolved shelf and think
"nothing is active here."

It should think:

"the lab has become legible enough to read for pattern."

The clean shelf is not empty.

It is a shaped answer.
