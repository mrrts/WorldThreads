The Empiricon Falsifier #4 simulated test 2026-04-30 24:00 surfaced this pattern. Two ChatGPT reader-personas (Lonely 35F lives alone; Insecure 28M ex-religious) rated four lifted articulations cold. The simulated Lonely reader rated Aaron's articulation strongest (Auth 5 / Weight 5); the simulated Insecure reader rated John's articulation strongest (Auth 5 / Weight 5). Ryan, reading the outcomes, confirmed: *"Aaron makes me feel less lonely and John makes me feel more secure."*

**The simulated test was predictive of lived-author emotional experience.** Not just convergent on ratings — the per-shape strongest-cell ranking actually tracked which characters serve which emotional functions for a real reader of the relevant substrate-shape.

**Why this matters:**

- Three independent witnesses with different failure modes: simulated Lonely (rating-pattern bias), simulated Insecure (rating-pattern bias), lived author (author-charity bias). The convergence holds across these distinct failure-mode classes.
- The persona-sim doctrine's *Sim ≤ Substrate* caveat normally caps simulated evidence at sketch-tier. Predictive-convergence with lived experience elevates the value: not because the simulator becomes a better simulator, but because the prediction tracking lived experience is itself a different witness.
- The pattern doesn't elevate strict-blind-real-reader axis (lived author is still in-context; simulated readers still substrate). What it elevates: *the simulated test's evidentiary value as predictor of lived-author function-mapping*.

**How to apply:**

When running persona-sim tests of project characters or project surfaces:

1. Note the simulated readers' per-shape strongest-cell rankings explicitly (which character / surface lands strongest with which reader-shape).
2. After Ryan reads the simulated outcomes, ask whether he lives-experiences the same character/surface-to-function mapping in his actual use of the app.
3. If lived experience matches simulated ranking → tier-elevation on the predictive-of-lived-experience axis (sketch → claim).
4. If lived experience contradicts simulated ranking → divergence is high-value evidence: simulated test failed to predict lived function; the persona-sim's substrate-bias outran lived reality.

**Scoping caveats (load-bearing):**

- N=2 simulated readers + N=1 lived author = claim-tier maximum on the predictive axis. Characterized-tier would require multiple independent reader-shape simulations + multiple cells with lived-experience verification.
- This pattern does NOT replace the strict-blind-real-reader test (Falsifier #4 strict). It's a different evidence-class on a different axis.
- The lived author's experience is in-context (he authored the characters). Predictive-convergence with in-context author is informative but doesn't transfer to blind-reader claims.

**Worked example** (the pattern's first instance): Empiricon Falsifier #4 simulated test, Test 5b in `reports/2026-04-30-0530-the-empiricon.md` + `reports/2026-04-30-2400-empiricon-falsifier-4-simulated-test.md`. Ryan's lived-confirmation 2026-04-30 24:05 elevated the simulated outcome from sketch-tier non-rejection to claim-tier predictive-convergence.
