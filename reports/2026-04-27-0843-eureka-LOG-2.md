**Discovery.** The deepest new law is methodological: the upgraded `/play` flow is no longer just a persona-sim feedback device. It has become a **differential instrument**. One branch predicts how a reader-shape expects the surface to land; another branch grounds against the live app pipeline; the difference between those branches is itself a first-class artifact.

**Why this is real.** The quintet's `DIVERGENT-BETTER` finding could not have existed without the two-branch structure. The persona-sims systematically underpredicted the live pipeline; Step 2.5 grounding surfaced that the prompt stack was doing work invisible to a fresh ChatGPT reader-sim. The methodology is no longer just "ask multiple personas." It is "branch the read, then learn from the delta."

**Why it matters.** This changes what counts as a good instrument in the repo. Single-branch impressions are easy to overread: a sim can mistake default-model weakness for app weakness; a single sympathetic encounter can hide friction another reader would catch. Differential instruments expose hidden lift, hidden drag, and hidden asymmetry. The project should prefer them when the stakes are real and the branching cost is cheap.
