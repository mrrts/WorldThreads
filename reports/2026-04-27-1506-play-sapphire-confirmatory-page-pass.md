## What this actually means

The proof layer is still doing real work. The lines readers trusted most were
the inspectable ones:

- `Bring your own OpenAI key.`
- `Keep your conversations on your own disk.`
- `Read the doctrine and prompt stack in the open.`

That has not reversed.

What changed is that, once the old trust-layer overreach and the old opener
genericity were reduced, the page's remaining pressure-point became easier to
see:

the **crown-register headline** itself.

Not because the page should stop being elevated, but because the current
headline still reads a little more like trailer-language than like earned
invitation for some readers.

And Jasper's read sharpens the secondary issue:

the explanatory body sentence is still doing too much backstage self-justifying
in one breath.

## The surprising friction

The new opener did not come back unanimously clean either.

`Characters with grain. Worlds with weight.` had just won the variant fight for
this page, but in this whole-page confirmatory pass the burned-by-AI skeptic
still flagged it as their remaining wince.

That does **not** overturn the prior finding. It means:

- the opener is much better than the old `real-feeling` line
- but it still sits within an invitation layer some wary readers experience as
  stylized before proof has fully landed

So the seam has narrowed, but not disappeared.

## The deeper read

This pass suggests the Sapphire problem is no longer:

- fake technical grandeur
- generic product-copy language
- telling the reader what they are seeking

Those were real and were improved.

The remaining problem is subtler:

**how much crown-language can the page carry before the reader has seen enough
ordinary daylight proof to consent to it?**

That is a better problem to have. It means the page is now losing on a narrower
and more interesting seam.

## Recommendation

Do **not** declare the Sapphire seam retired yet.

The next honest move is to treat the headline itself as the new live fight:

- keep the crown-image, but test whether `as the world answers back` is still
  spending trust too quickly
- optionally test whether `Crown-Grade Convergence` is helping or merely
  preloading significance
- after that, if the headline holds, tighten the long `Underneath...` sentence
  so it says less about the mechanism while preserving the anti-mush /
  anti-fake-intimacy truth

The proof layer is now strong enough that the page does not need to be
dramatically rewritten again. It needs one more micro-discrimination pass
higher up the page.

## Lesson learned

Once lower-page trust gets cleaner, the remaining overreach does not disappear.

It **rises**.

The top-of-page invitation becomes easier to hear clearly.
