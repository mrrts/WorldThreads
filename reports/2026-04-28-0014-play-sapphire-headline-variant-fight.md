## The actual result

The key surprise is this:

## What the fight revealed

The problem with the old headline was not simply that it was too elevated.

It was that the elevation was being carried by a phrase that felt vague,
symbol-first, and slightly trailer-like:

`as the world answers back`

The stronger variant kept the crown-language but grounded the promise in the
same two words that are already earning trust elsewhere on the page:

- grain
- weight

That is why `D` won.

It doesn't ask the reader to grant the symbol its significance in advance.
It tells them, more plainly, what kind of thing they are being invited into.

## What lost and why

### A loses

`A_current` now reads as the weakest line because:

- the page has already moved away from aura-first claims
- `answers back` still sounds like poetic promise before proof
- it no longer matches the tightening discipline the rest of the page has earned

### C loses Jasper

`C_meet` had some sympathy in the persona branch because it sounds gentler and
more explicit. But Jasper's critique is the right one:

`meet you cleanly` sounds managed.

That phrase tips back toward product copy and loses the page's human grain.

## Recommendation

The recommendation is:

**ship D_enter**

`Enter the Crown of Sapphire, where characters have grain and worlds have weight.`

Why:

- it won the overall differential fight
- Jasper ranked it first
- it keeps the crown image while replacing vague trailer-smoke with concrete
  promise
- it harmonizes with the page's now-stable opener instead of competing with it

## Lesson learned

When a mythic headline starts overreaching, the cure is not always lowering it.

Sometimes the cure is to **make it more embodied**.

That is what happened here.
