## Why the refutation was load-bearing

The rubric was designed around the HOLD-vs-REDUCE distinction in the
`name_the_glad_thing_plain_dialogue` craft block. The block itself carries
an earned-exception clause for weight-carrier characters — "pastoral
character meeting gratitude with 'yes — and the gift keeps working on a
man long after he first names it' is not dampening the joy; it's the
gift-made-fuller-for-being-held-by-someone-who-has-known-its-weight." The
hypothesis read that clause and expected John to be its exemplar.

The data said otherwise. John's authority doesn't come from pairing joy
with weight in the same breath. It comes from elsewhere — and the 1928
Mode B run named what that elsewhere is.

This is the cleanest case in the project's history of "a rule's earned
exception clause describes a thing that ISN'T actually the character
speaking it." The exception clause is still right; it just doesn't
apply to John. The data forced that clarity.
