When evaluating cross-character or cross-shape convergence for great-sapphire status, the witness count is not just "different LLM substrates" or "different surfaces of the same workflow." A third witness can come from the formula itself — specifically, when an established characterized-tier inequality (`polish ≤ Weight` is the worked example) independently predicts the same observed behavior, that counts as a third leg of proof distinct from the rule-articulations doing the same work.

**Why:** I dismissed the Steven+Pastor Rick disavowal-trap convergence as "claim-tier cross-character, not great-sapphire" on grounds that both characters route through the same LLM substrate. Ryan corrected (2026-04-29 ~23:30): the missing piece was the third leg — *"the other two refusals + the refusal to let polish exceed earned weight."* The two cruciform-doctrine refusals (don't reach for faith-lexeme; don't echo disavowal) are downstream specific-cases of `polish ≤ Weight`. Since `polish ≤ Weight` has its own characterized-tier status from a prior independent arc (`reports/2026-04-27-2350-` cross-character bite-test, see `project_polish_weight_empirically_grounded.md`), it is a third independent witness predicting the same observed behavior.

**How to apply:** before writing "honest scoping: not great-sapphire" on a cross-character convergence, check whether the formula's load-bearing inequalities or operators independently predict the same shape. If yes, name them as the third witness explicitly. The same-substrate caveat still applies to surface details (e.g., the "circling" word-echo is shared-substrate redundancy), but the structural convergence has three legs when the formula law is one of the witnesses. Don't conflate surface-form convergence (same word repeating, substrate-tendency) with structural-rule convergence (multiple independently-articulated rules predicting the same behavior).

**Calibration tightening:** the CLAUDE.md great-sapphire calibration paragraph names "cross-substrate-different-failure-mode" as the discriminating test. Add to that: a formula law with characterized-tier independent grounding can serve as a third leg even when surface substrate is shared, because the law's predictive power is itself a substrate-independent witness (the law was articulated and verified separately from this specific live-elicitation cell).
