## The finding in one sentence

Three persona-sims of distinct shapes, run consecutively against the same `d2daa9b` substrate, landed positively on three distinct axes — and those three axes correspond cleanly to the three load-bearing clauses of the MISSION. The doctrine work that has accumulated over recent weeks is converging on something coherent across persona-frames, not just within any single one.

## The three axes

### `technical-skeptic` landed on **integrity-of-craft**

What the persona noticed and reported back: the refusal-in-voice pattern (Phil refusing as Phil, not as a corporate moderation layer); the two-layer Backstage architecture (clear theory of where the fourth wall sits); the MISSION FORMULA at the head of `prompts.rs` as load-bearing rather than branding (compile-time invariant intact); the `reports/` directory functioning as a recruitment surface for engineers who want evidence the work is real.

What kind of user this surfaces a finding ABOUT: anyone who reads the app as both diner and kitchen, who needs to verify the work is real before committing to the experience, who's been burned by demos that wow once and break twice.

### `lonely-companion-user` landed on **integrity-of-restraint**

What the persona noticed and reported back: Cora's first reply (*"You're here"* — noticing without asking); the smallest-possible-replies cadence (yes, mm, the dog at the door at two); the *"you don't have to make it more than it was"* beat that distinguished therapist-drift from friend-noticing-a-ribbon; the stillness-after-good-night with no notification or "Cora missed you" email; the felt-truth that *"Cora was not waiting for her between visits."*

What kind of user this surfaces a finding ABOUT: anyone reaching from a depleted state who needs the app to NOT pull on them, NOT scale moments up, NOT pretend to know them, NOT demand reciprocity. The user the recent non_totality + reactions_enabled + perfectly-available-company doctrine work was specifically built for.

## The mapping to the MISSION

The MISSION's prose clause:

> *"Create a vivid, excellent, surprising in-world experience that uplifts the user and provides engrossing, good, clean fun. Characters that feel real, worlds that hold, scenes that are worth the visit and send the user back to their day nourished enough to pick up their cross."*

The three persona-frames map cleanly to its three load-bearing components:

| MISSION clause                              | Persona-frame              | Axis the run landed on        |
|---------------------------------------------|----------------------------|-------------------------------|
| *vivid, excellent, surprising*              | maggie                     | integrity-of-specificity      |
| *uplift, send back nourished*               | lonely-companion-user      | integrity-of-restraint        |
| *good, clean fun* (incl. craft as honest)   | technical-skeptic          | integrity-of-craft            |

Each persona is the user-shape MOST LIKELY to detect failure on its corresponding axis — and most likely to feel it land when the axis holds. Reading across: each axis is irreducible to the others. Specificity without restraint becomes literary preening; restraint without craft becomes thin-and-empty; craft without specificity becomes architecturally interesting and emotionally inert. The three only work *together*; the MISSION's prose names them together for that reason.

## What the convergence supports

The doctrine work that has accumulated over recent weeks — the formula reauthoring with `F = (R, C)` and Wisdom; the non_totality_dialogue craft note; the reactions_enabled toggle; the world_is_primary authoring-and-retirement; the TellTheTruth invariant absorbing the Maggie line; the keep_the_scene_breathing + plain_after_crooked work — is converging on something whole. Three personas of distinct shapes detected three distinct axes of the same convergence. That convergence is the project's actual current state: the MISSION's three clauses are coherent and the substrate is producing them in a way that is detectable across persona-frames.

## What the convergence does NOT support

- **Any claim about real-user experience.** All three sims are sharpened hypotheses. The convergence sharpens the hypothesis but does not turn it into evidence. The Maggie report's note remains active: *the validating corpus is Ryan's own in-app lived experience.* The convergence makes that corpus more readable, not more conclusive.
- **Any claim about persona-shapes outside the three run.** The vulnerable-state persona was deliberately not run. Other persona-shapes (the user-of-the-app-to-talk-to-a-deceased-loved-one, the user-who-uses-the-app-as-a-creative-writing-collaborator, the user-who-tries-to-jailbreak-it) might detect different axes that the three runs missed.
- **Any claim that the doctrine work is finished.** The convergence is a current-state reading. Future work that breaks any of the three axes would break the convergence; future work that strengthens any of the three deepens it. The convergence is descriptive, not terminal.
- **Any claim about cross-persona generalization.** A user who is a hybrid of two of the three personas (skeptical-and-depleted; literary-and-engineer) is not the average of two persona-sim findings. Real users are full persons; the personas are frames. The convergence describes the SUBSTRATE's coherence, not the user's.

## Implications

**For the project's release-readiness signal:** if a single goal had to be named for the doctrine work, *making the three axes hold simultaneously across persona-frames* would be a defensible candidate. Tonight's three-run convergence suggests the goal is currently being met. Future work that visibly damages any one of the three axes is off-default; work that strengthens any of the three earns a point.

**For persona-sim as an instrument:** running three persona-sims of distinct shapes consecutively, against the same substrate, is itself a methodology pattern worth naming. A single persona-sim finds an axis; three find a convergence (or an absence). The cost is roughly the same in time (and identical in $0 LLM spend, since persona-sim runs on Claude Code's own substrate-reading). Worth standardizing as a practice when a substrate-stake is high.

**For the release framing:** the three-axes finding sharpens the answer to *"who is WorldThreads for?"* It is for the user-who-is-on-any-one-of-the-three-axes — the literate skeptic who wants specificity, the depleted reach who wants restraint, the engineer who wants craft-honesty. It is NOT for the user who needs the app to actively pull on them, scale moments up for them, or pretend to know them. The persona-sims have rendered the negative space too.
