# /eureka — middleware splits into compositional and epistemic

## Discovery

The middleware family wants the same sharpening that control-plane truth just got.

The discovery is:

- **compositional middleware**
- **epistemic middleware**

That is the cleaner split.

## Why the split is real

The project already named several middleware types:

- trajectory
- copy
- interaction
- criterion
- hypothesis
- rubric-ref carry

Those are not all doing the same kind of work.

Some primarily shape the next act of making.

Others primarily carry forward what later evidence or loops are allowed to mean or require.

That is not a minor distinction. It is the family dividing along two different steering questions.

## Child 1 — compositional middleware

Compositional middleware asks:

> what should the next composition be shaped by?

Worked examples:

- **trajectory middleware** — steers what gets written next at the session arc
- **copy middleware** — steers the next sentence
- **interaction middleware** — steers the next control-shape move

These all govern the next emission or next design move directly.

## Child 2 — epistemic middleware

Epistemic middleware asks:

> what methodological boundary or durable carrier is now being brought forward into later evidence-work?

Worked examples:

- **criterion middleware** — carries corrected meaning-boundaries forward
- **hypothesis middleware** — carries durable question-shape and loop discipline forward
- **rubric-ref carry** — carries prior correction into later evaluator runs

These do not mainly tell the next sentence what to be. They tell later evidence-work what it is allowed to claim, inherit, or owe.

## Why this matters

Without the split, "middleware" stays useful but blurrier than it needs to be.

With the split, the diagnosis improves:

- if the surface is steering the next act of making, it is a compositional instrument
- if the surface is carrying forward a methodological boundary or question-shape, it is an epistemic instrument

That also sharpens enforcement choices.

Compositional middleware tends to want:

- auto-fire
- hook placement
- loop insertion
- late-slot wording discipline

Epistemic middleware tends to want:

- durable fields
- registry shape
- rubric-library reuse
- explicit resolution/provenance surfaces

Same doctrine class. Different intervention family.

## Practical test

The practical discriminator is:

> if the surface mainly shapes the next emission, it is compositional middleware
> if the surface mainly shapes the meaning or discipline of later evidence, it is epistemic middleware

That is the sharp split.

## Outcome

The middleware family now reads more cleanly:

- **middleware** — parent doctrine class
- **compositional middleware** — child for next-emission steering
- **epistemic middleware** — child for carried-forward methodological constraint

That is a better map than leaving all middleware under one undifferentiated heading.
