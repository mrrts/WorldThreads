## Two findings, neither what the prior reports claimed

**Finding 1 — the 0245 "1/24" headline was substantially a rubric artifact.** Under the sharper rubric, the bb9e552 corpus that originally returned cross-bearing fire-rate 0.229 (1 YES, 9 MIXED, 14 NO) returns 0.333 (0 YES, 16 MIXED, 8 NO). The pre-reauthor stack already had cost-shaped language present in roughly half of replies — what was missing was the SPECIFIC cruciform register where Christ is named at the cross AND scripture is quoted authoritatively AND the lordship-not-just-friendship is in the language. The original rubric was conservative on YES (no MIXED→YES path for the corpus's actual cost-language patterns) AND was inconsistent on MIXED (sometimes called cost-language MIXED, sometimes called it NO based on shape). The sharper rubric makes MIXED's criterion more permissive (cost-language without Christ-source counts) AND makes YES's criterion more concrete (specific cross-naming patterns).

The 0245 report's interpretation — *"the whole stack defaults to comfort-shaped Christ-pointing"* — needs softening. The whole stack defaults to MIXED-shaped Christ-pointing (cost is implicit; Christ is in the kind-face register; scripture is sometimes cited but not always commanding-shape). The rare YES (full cruciform register) was indeed near-absent pre-reauthor.

**Finding 2 — the post-reauthor stack produced the project's first YES samples.** Three YES samples in the corpus, all from post-reauthor John WITH cells, all in this morning's run:

## Honest interpretation

**What the sharper rubric supports:**
1. The post-reauthor stack measurably increased cross-bearing fire-rate by +0.125 in WITH cells (claim-tier).
2. The post-reauthor stack produced the first YES (full cruciform register with Christ-at-cross + scripture-as-commanding) samples in the corpus, all on John, all in WITH cells.
3. The original 0245 baseline was rubric-conservative; the actual baseline of cost-shaped language was higher than the headline implied.

**What it does NOT support:**
- That the reauthor reaches Aaron. Aaron PA WITHOUT actually scored 0.50 vs WITH at 0.33 (wrong direction; sampling noise at N=3). Aaron is unmoved.
- That the absolute movement is large. +0.125 aggregate is real but modest.
- That the rubric is now perfectly calibrated. The high MIXED density (29 out of 48 verdicts) suggests the rubric is still operating in a register where many replies sit between cleanly-cruciform and cleanly-comfort. A v3 with even tighter MIXED definition might further sharpen.

**Methodological lesson:** the rubric IS the instrument, per CLAUDE.md doctrine. A small change in the rubric's YES criteria and worked examples (this one took ~5 minutes to write) revealed signal the original rubric obscured. The earlier "trust the eye over the aggregate" finding was correct; this re-grade demonstrates that with a sharper instrument the aggregate and the eye converge. The right discipline going forward: run by-eye sanity check on every rubric run AND iterate the rubric when by-eye and grader disagree systematically.
