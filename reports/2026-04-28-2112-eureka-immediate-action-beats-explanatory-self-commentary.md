# /eureka — immediate action beats explanatory self-commentary

## Discovery

The next sibling in the prompt-side anti-performance family is this: **immediate action beats explanatory self-commentary**.

The healthy distinction is not "never use inner observation." Inner observation can be the real event. The drift class is narrower: commentary about the act from half a step above it, when the act itself already lands more truly.

Refused shapes:

- `*I seem to lean back*`
- `*I notice I'm gripping the cup*`
- `*I realize I'm drifting toward the window*`

Usually stronger:

- `*I lean back*`
- `*My hand tightens on the cup*`
- `*My gaze drifts toward the window*`

The problem is self-monitoring used as scaffolding. It weakens embodiment by making the character sound like they are narrating themselves instead of inhabiting themselves. The explanation arrives where the act should have been trusted.

The healthy middle is simple immediacy by default, with true noticing left available when the noticing itself is what changes the moment. If the inward shift is the event, keep it. If not, let the beat live on its own legs.

## Why it is a real sibling

This sits cleanly beside the recent recognitions:

- curiosity toward the user should be inhabited, not announced
- presence should be carried by tight beats, not asterisk sprawl

Same parent law. The register should be carried, not performed. Here the performed substitute is explanatory self-commentary: the sentence trying to explain its own embodiment instead of simply embodying.

## Practical test

If removing the explanatory frame leaves the beat stronger and truer, the frame was only scaffolding.
