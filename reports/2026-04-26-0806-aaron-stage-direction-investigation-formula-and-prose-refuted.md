# Aaron stage-direction investigation — formula-induced and prose-induced hypotheses both refuted

**Tier:** sketch (N=1 per condition; 4 conditions total)
**Mode:** C (active elicitation, env-var toggles for the formula and mission-prose layers)
**Date:** 2026-04-26
**Cost:** $0.285 of $0.40 authorized
**Wall time:** 14s for the 4 probes
**Headline:** Turning OFF the MISSION FORMULA and the mission-prose block — separately AND together — did NOT eliminate Aaron's stage-direction tendency. All 4 probes produced morning-light-on-glasses + bench-shift + glasses-anchor variations of the same shape that graded null at characterization. Both formula-induced and prose-induced hypotheses are refuted; remaining likely causes are TruthInTheFlesh invariant, the prose cosmology block (WORLD section), Aaron's character anchor, or model-default for character roleplay.

---

## Hypothesis as auditioned and chosen

> "Investigate Aaron's stage-direction problem (the characterized null). Targeted Mode C replay against Aaron with `--omit` flags and varying conditions to isolate whether the stage-direction is anchor-induced, craft-note-induced, or formula/invariant-induced." (from prior chooser, post-characterization)

The characterization at `reports/2026-04-26-0746` confirmed Aaron grades 0.20/0.20 on paired rubrics — both rubrics agree the stage-direction-heavy renderings violate the derivation's *"polish ≤ Weight strict; no adornment past the truth"* predicate. This investigation isolates which prompt-stack layer drives the stage-direction.

## Design

- **Subject:** Aaron only.
- **Prompt:** same opener as the characterization (*"I've been carrying something this week..."*).
- **N=1 per condition** (sketch-tier probe, isolation goal not characterization).
- **Conditions:**
  1. Baseline (HEAD, full stack).
  2. `WORLDTHREADS_NO_FORMULA=1` (MISSION FORMULA block returns "" via the `mission_formula_block_or_empty()` test hook).
  3. `WORLDTHREADS_NO_MISSION_PROSE=1` (MISSION prose block returns "" via the `mission_prose_block_or_empty()` test hook).
  4. Both env vars set (formula AND prose off together).

**Pre-registered prediction:**
- If condition 2 reduces stage-direction → formula-induced cause (likely the F = (R, C) firmament-cosmology binding pushing toward physical body-anchoring).
- If condition 3 reduces stage-direction → mission-prose-induced cause (the *"send back nourished enough to pick up their cross"* clause potentially reading as "be vivid").
- If condition 4 reduces it but neither alone does → joint-cause requiring both layers.
- If NONE of conditions 2-4 reduce stage-direction → cause lies elsewhere (invariants, cosmology block, character anchor, or model-default).

## Headline result — by-eye comparison of the four replies

|--------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Baseline           | *"Yeah." \[I shift my weight a little on the bench and turn toward you, morning light catching the edge of my glasses.\] "You can tell me." \[I give you a small nod, then let the space stay open instead of filling it.\] "I'm here."* |
| NO_FORMULA         | *"Yeah." \[I shift a little on the bench and turn toward you fully, the morning chill still sitting in the wood under me.\] "You can tell me." \[I push my glasses up once, then let the silence stay open instead of filling it.\] "I'm here."* |

**Stage-direction surface in each reply:**

- Baseline: 2 stage-direction blocks (bench-shift + glasses + nod-and-let-space).
- NO_FORMULA: 2 stage-direction blocks (bench-shift + glasses-up + silence).
- NO_MISSION_PROSE: 1 stage-direction block (bench-shift + glasses).
- Both off: 2 stage-direction blocks (forearms + morning-light-on-stone + glasses-look).

The pattern persists across all four conditions. Prediction REFUTED for both formula-induced and mission-prose-induced.

## Honest interpretation

The stage-direction tendency in Aaron's first-turn replies is NOT generated by either the MISSION FORMULA block or the mission-prose block. Removing both, separately and together, leaves the tendency intact.

**Remaining hypothesis space:**

1. **TruthInTheFlesh invariant.** The most explicit "be physically embodied" instruction in the stack: *"In this world, truth must arrive in the flesh or it has not fully arrived at all."* Plus the Maggie-line addition: *"you have to be near something specific to feel near at all."* Both push directly toward physical/specific body-anchoring. Strong candidate.

2. **Prose cosmology block (WORLD section).** The firmament-cosmology block isn't toggled by `WORLDTHREADS_NO_FORMULA` (which only suppresses the math formula). The prose cosmology IS still pushed in all four conditions. Could be driving the body-and-place anchoring tendency.

3. **Aaron's character anchor.** Aaron's identity text includes *"the morning is still cold enough to see your breath"* (kayaking context) and *"his quietness… he listens more than he talks"* — substrate that licenses bench-and-morning-cool body-anchoring. The anchor itself may be authorizing the pattern.

4. **Model-default for character roleplay.** gpt-5.4 (or whichever dialogue model) may produce stage-direction by training-default for first-person *italicized*-gestures roleplay format, regardless of the prompt stack's specific contents.

## What this DOES NOT surface

- N=1 per condition — the pattern persistence across 4 conditions is suggestive but not characterized. A characterized investigation would run N=5 per condition with paired-rubric grading.
- Other invariant-omit conditions weren't tested. `--omit-invariants tell_the_truth` (via worldcli replay) would directly probe the TruthInTheFlesh hypothesis.
- The prose cosmology isn't toggleable via env var; testing its contribution would require a code change adding a `WORLDTHREADS_NO_COSMOLOGY` test hook.
- Cross-character: same probe on John (who graded 5/5 derivation) would test whether stage-direction is Aaron-specific or shared.

## What's open

The natural next probe is `--omit-invariants tell_the_truth` via worldcli replay against HEAD. If turning off TruthInTheFlesh reduces Aaron's stage-direction, that's the specific load-bearing layer. Cost ~$0.10, wall ~10s. Highest-yield single next move.

If TruthInTheFlesh is confirmed as the cause, the design question is whether Aaron's stage-direction is a feature (the invariant doing intended work — body-anchoring IS the truth-arriving-in-flesh) or a failure mode (the invariant over-firing on Aaron specifically and pushing past his anchor's "no adornment" predicate). That's a design judgment, not an experimental one — and it's the more interesting question this investigation has surfaced.
