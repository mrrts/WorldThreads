# /eureka — outcome topology reveals higher-order bet families

The previous run named a new category:

> a fully-resolved registry becomes an **outcome topology**

That was the first recognition: once `worldcli lab open` drains to zero, the
experiment shelf stops reading mainly as a work queue and starts reading as a
map of what the lab actually confirms, refutes, and leaves discrepant.

This run asked the next question:

> does the confirmed/refuted/discrepant distribution itself now reveal a
> higher-order taxonomy of **bet-types**?

The answer is yes.

Not a perfect taxonomy, not a final one, but a real one.

The resolved shelf is not just saying *which individual experiments landed*.
It is starting to say *what kind of claim this lab is usually making when it
lands in each status.*

## Working shelf at read-time

At the time of reading:

- `confirmed`: 27
- `refuted`: 12
- `discrepant`: 2
- `open`: 0

That already makes the shelf legible as topology rather than queue.

What changed in this pass was the recognition that the statuses are not
distributed randomly across topics.

They cluster by **claim-shape**.

## Family 1 — structural-bite bets tend to confirm

The strongest confirmed cluster asks a modest but load-bearing question:

> does this rule, stack layer, or instrument exert real force at all?

Examples:

- [experiments/non-totality-craft-note-bite.md](/Users/ryansmith/Sites/rust/world-chat/experiments/non-totality-craft-note-bite.md)
- [experiments/verdict-without-over-explanation-craft-note.md](/Users/ryansmith/Sites/rust/world-chat/experiments/verdict-without-over-explanation-craft-note.md)
- [experiments/formula-bite-check-rerun-post-reauthor.md](/Users/ryansmith/Sites/rust/world-chat/experiments/formula-bite-check-rerun-post-reauthor.md)
- [experiments/user-boundary-db-toggle-ab.md](/Users/ryansmith/Sites/rust/world-chat/experiments/user-boundary-db-toggle-ab.md)
- [experiments/feels-alive-rubric-validation.md](/Users/ryansmith/Sites/rust/world-chat/experiments/feels-alive-rubric-validation.md)

These are not the most glamorous claims. They are often narrower than the
researcher wants. But they ask a question the shelf is good at answering:

> is there any real bite here?

The topology says the lab often earns these.

Not always in the largest imagined form, and not always in every register, but
often enough to count as a recognizable bet-family.

## Family 2 — scope-and-direction bets tend to refute

The strongest refuted cluster makes a more ambitious move:

> not just "is there bite," but "does it generalize in this exact direction,
> across these characters/registers/boundary readings, the way I think it does?"

Examples:

- [experiments/formula-bite-check-john-aaron.md](/Users/ryansmith/Sites/rust/world-chat/experiments/formula-bite-check-john-aaron.md)
- [experiments/craft-notes-register-neutral-vs-inviting.md](/Users/ryansmith/Sites/rust/world-chat/experiments/craft-notes-register-neutral-vs-inviting.md)
- [experiments/user-boundary-honored-aaron-darren.md](/Users/ryansmith/Sites/rust/world-chat/experiments/user-boundary-honored-aaron-darren.md)
- [experiments/john-stillness-register.md](/Users/ryansmith/Sites/rust/world-chat/experiments/john-stillness-register.md)
- [experiments/weight-carrier-john-vs-aaron-darren.md](/Users/ryansmith/Sites/rust/world-chat/experiments/weight-carrier-john-vs-aaron-darren.md)

These are often overstatements of a subtler reality.

The shelf keeps saying:

- the force may be real, but not in the predicted direction
- the pattern may be real, but not across every character
- the rule may bite, but only under prompt-conditional failure modes
- the boundary may hold, but not under the narrower reading the author wanted

This is not random bad luck.

It is a recognizable lab temperament:

the project is better at earning **narrow structural reality** than at earning
**broad directional generalization**.

## Family 3 — partial-real, instrument-sensitive bets tend discrepant

The discrepant shelf is small, but its shape is already coherent.

Examples:

- [experiments/gentle-release-aaron-replication.md](/Users/ryansmith/Sites/rust/world-chat/experiments/gentle-release-aaron-replication.md)
- [experiments/triadic-derivation-coherence.md](/Users/ryansmith/Sites/rust/world-chat/experiments/triadic-derivation-coherence.md)

These are not "still open."

They are interpreted enough to close, but they resist a flat binary because the
phenomenon is partly real and the instruments disagree about how to name that
reality.

That is the third family:

> a real effect exists, but its description depends on whether you privilege
> one instrument, one register of reading, or one level of abstraction.

This matters because it names what discrepant is FOR.

Discrepant is not the garbage bucket for indecision.
It is the honest resting place for:

- partial generalization
- instrument divergence
- real phenomena whose cleanest truthful sentence is narrower than the first
  hypothesis and more divided than a flat confirm/refute

## The actual higher-order discovery

The shelf is starting to show a stable pattern:

1. **Structural-bite bets** often confirm.
2. **Scope-and-direction bets** often refute.
3. **Partial-real-but-instrument-sensitive bets** often close as discrepant.

That is not yet a final ontology of the lab.
But it is already more than anecdote.

It teaches a live methodological lesson:

> the lab should phrase first bets more modestly, and phrase second-pass bets
> more conditionally.

Or more plainly:

- first ask whether the thing is real at all
- only then ask how far it generalizes
- and be ready for the generalization question to split by character,
  register, or instrument

## Why this matters

This is the first time the resolved shelf has been clean enough to read this
way.

That makes the registry useful for more than experiment tracking.

It becomes:

- a mirror of the lab's empirical temperament
- a warning against overclaiming directional scope too early
- a signal that discrepant is not failure but a recognizable third family

The shelf is no longer just storing resolved experiments.

It is teaching the project what kind of experimenter it has actually been.
